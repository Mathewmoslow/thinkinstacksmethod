What is TISM?

The Think In Stacks Method is a structured approach to prioritizing nursing interventions based on a four-stack hierarchy:

  1. Life Threats (ABC+D) - Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability
  2. Safety - Falls, Infection, Violence
  3. Physical Needs - Glucose (minutes), Pain/Elimination (hours), Nutrition (days)
  4. Nursing Process - Assessment, Diagnosis, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation

Remember: Assessment wins ties!

ðŸ“Š Validation Results

TISM has been validated on 68 priority-setting NCLEX questions with the following results:

  • Basic TISM: 25.0% accuracy (+2.9% over random baseline)
  • Context-Aware: 26.5% accuracy (+4.4% over baseline)
  • With Clinical Knowledge: 27.9% accuracy (+5.9% over baseline)
  • Clear emergencies: Up to 100% accuracy

Key Finding: TISM works best as a thinking framework that organizes clinical knowledge, not as a standalone answer-finder.
